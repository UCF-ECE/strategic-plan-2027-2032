Mission & Vision

Vision

To be a nationally recognized department that produces technically excellent, professionally adaptable engineers while advancing knowledge across the full spectrum of electrical and computer engineering—celebrated for the quality of its graduates, the impact of its research, and the innovative programs that distinguish it from peer institutions.

Mission

ECE advances its mission by: (1) delivering a rigorous, industry-aligned curriculum that evolves with technological change; (2) preparing graduates for workforce impact through experiential learning, online education, and HIPs; (3) conducting research that generates knowledge, IP, and commercial value; (4) cultivating a culture that recognizes excellence in teaching, research, service, and entrepreneurship; and (5) sustaining partnerships with industry, alumni, defense agencies, and national laboratories.

Resources & Acquisition

Existing Resources & Opportunities
→Strong enrollment generating tuition-based resources
→Nationally competitive faculty with active NSF, DoD, DOE, and industry funding
→Geographic advantage: defense/aerospace corridor (L3Harris, Lockheed, Northrop, NAVWAR)
→Existing online infrastructure extensible to new tracks; institutional revenue-sharing support
→Faculty with joint appointments at INL and interdisciplinary cluster affiliations
→UCF semiconductor initiative and CHIPS Act national momentum
→AI-for-ECE committee with chair support and incentivized adoption budget
→FFL program with explicit Dean endorsement as replicable model
→Established CDL and CCIE/STE connections for faculty certification
→Active faculty outreach through LIFE@UCF and community channels
→Student chapter infrastructure available for reactivation
Additional Resources Needed
→≥1 dedicated AI/IT support staff for digital infrastructure and lab platforms
→≥20 ULA positions to support faculty AI-integration efforts
→Faculty training/workshop budget: 2–3 domain-specific workshops/year
→Flexible learning infrastructure: digital-twin, remote-lab HIL, AR/VR, vendor partnerships
→3–5 additional tenure-track faculty lines over 5 years
→Increased GTA/GRA lines for PhD enrollment growth and teaching
→Lab modernization funding ($500K–$1M over 5 years)
→Additional research and teaching space (formal space-needs analysis)
→Student chapter support: ~$10K–$15K/year for conference travel
→Entrepreneurship Laureate seed funding: stipends, pitch prizes, fees
→EDA license expansion and shared GPU cluster or cloud allocation
