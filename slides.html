Mission & Vision
Where We're Headed

Vision

To be a nationally recognized department that produces technically excellent, professionally adaptable engineers while advancing knowledge across the full spectrum of ECE — celebrated for the quality of its graduates, the impact of its research, and the innovative programs that distinguish it from peer institutions.

Mission — Five Commitments

  • Deliver a rigorous, industry-aligned curriculum that evolves with technology
  • Prepare graduates through experiential learning, online education, & high-impact practices
  • Conduct research that generates knowledge, IP, and commercial value
  • Cultivate a culture recognizing excellence in teaching, research, service, & entrepreneurship
  • Sustain partnerships with industry, alumni, defense agencies, & national labs

Priority 1 · Goals
Three Goals for Student Success
G1
Flexible Learning Environments — Expand online/mixed-mode (Robotics MS, NASA Parts Certificate), build remote-access lab infrastructure (digital twins, AR/VR), train faculty via CDL/CCIE certification.
G2
Exponential Technology Curve — AI across the "ECE 2030 curriculum" via closed-loop (Implement → Assess → Revise), faculty incentives, ULA support, progressive critical-thinking assignments.
G3
Industry–Curriculum Gap — IAB annual reviews, new certificates (Semiconductor Mfg, Hardware Security, AI-for-EE), HIPs to 75%+, grow student professional chapters.
Year 1
Pilots launched · Faculty certified · IAB gap survey · Chapters reactivated
Year 3
1 online MS · 30%+ AI courses · +15pp HIP · 5+ chapters
Year 5
≥2 online programs · 60%+ AI pedagogy · 75%+ HIP

Three Goals for Discovery
G1
Grow & Revamp Labs — Comprehensive audit, phased modernization ($250K–$1M), new labs in AI/ML hardware, semiconductor, power electronics, robotics. Update senior design with modern EDA, FPGA, embedded ML.
